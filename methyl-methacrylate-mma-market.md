<p><p>Methyl methacrylate (MMA) is produced through three main methods: Acetone cyanohydrin (ACH) method, Isobutylene method, and Ethylene method. The ACH method involves the reaction of acetone and hydrogen cyanide, while the Isobutylene method utilizes isobutylene and formaldehyde as raw materials. The Ethylene method involves the direct oxidation of ethylene. Each method has its own advantages and disadvantages in terms of cost, efficiency, and environmental impact, leading to varying market preferences among manufacturers.</p></p>
