<p><p>Aliphatic polyglycols are a type of polymer that consists of repeating units of glycol molecules. The main types of aliphatic polyglycols are Polyethylene Glycol (PEG), Polypropylene Glycol (PPG), and Polytetrahydrofuran (PTHF). PEG is widely used in pharmaceuticals, personal care products, and industrial applications. PPG is commonly used in lubricants, hydraulic fluids, and cosmetics. PTHF is known for its flexibility and is used in the production of spandex fibers. Each type of polyglycol has specific properties that make them suitable for different applications.</p></p>
