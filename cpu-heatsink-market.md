<p><p>The CPU heatsink market consists of three main types: air cooling, water cooling, and thermoelectric cooling heatsinks. Air cooling heatsinks use fans to dissipate heat, making them popular for their simplicity and affordability. Water cooling heatsinks rely on water to absorb and transfer heat away from the CPU, offering more efficient cooling performance. Thermoelectric cooling heatsinks use electricity to create a temperature difference, effectively cooling the CPU. Each type caters to different consumer needs and preferences in terms of cooling efficiency and cost.</p></p>
