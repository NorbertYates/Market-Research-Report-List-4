<p><p>Acoustic wave sensors are widely used in biology for various applications. Two main types in the market are Quartz Crystal Microbalance (QCM) Sensor and Surface Acoustic Wave (SAW) Sensor. QCM sensors measure mass changes on a quartz crystal surface by monitoring frequency shifts. SAW sensors use acoustic waves to detect binding events on their surface. Both sensors are highly sensitive and can be used for a range of biological applications such as detecting biomolecules, pathogens, and monitoring cell behavior.</p></p>
