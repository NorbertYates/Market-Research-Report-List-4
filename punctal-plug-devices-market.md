Temporary punctal plugs are made of dissolvable materials and provide short-term relief. Permanent punctal plugs are made of non-absorbable materials and offer long-lasting effects. Temporary plugs are often used for initial treatment or testing purposes, while permanent plugs are recommended for chronic cases. Both types of plugs help improve tear retention and alleviate dry eye symptoms.</p></p>
