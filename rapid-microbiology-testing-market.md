<p><p>Rapid microbiology testing can be categorized into different types based on the method used for testing. Growth-based methods rely on observing the growth of microorganisms in a specific environment. Viability-based methods determine the viability of microorganisms by assessing metabolic activity or membrane integrity. Cellular component-based methods target specific cellular components for identification. Nucleic acid-based methods detect genetic material for identification. Other methods include immunological assays, biochemical tests, and others. Each method offers unique advantages for rapid and accurate microbiology testing.</p></p>
