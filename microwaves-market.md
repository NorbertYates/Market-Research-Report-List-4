<p><p>Microwaves come in two main market types: Built-in and Counter Top. Built-in microwaves are designed to be integrated into cabinetry or wall units, providing a seamless and streamlined look in the kitchen. They are often more expensive than countertop models due to their specialized installation requirements. Counter top microwaves, on the other hand, are standalone units that sit on the kitchen counter. They are more affordable and versatile, as they can easily be moved around or replaced as needed. Both types offer convenience and efficiency in cooking and reheating food.</p></p>
