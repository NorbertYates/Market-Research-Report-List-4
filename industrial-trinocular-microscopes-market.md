<p><p>Industrial trinocular microscopes are classified into two main types: inverted type and upright type. Inverted type microscopes have the light source below the specimen, making them ideal for observing samples that require a larger working distance. On the other hand, upright type microscopes have the light source above the specimen, providing a more traditional viewing angle. Both types are commonly used in industrial applications for magnifying and analyzing small samples, but their design and features cater to different needs and preferences.</p></p>
