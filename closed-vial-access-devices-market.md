There are two main types of closed vial access devices - with needle and needleless. Closed vial access devices with needle involve piercing the vial septum with a needle to withdraw medication, while needleless devices utilize a blunt cannula to access the vial without the use of a needle. Both types offer benefits such as reducing the risk of needlestick injuries and contamination.</p></p>
